The Operator Behind the Consulting

Why I do this.
And why it works.

For 19 years I stood inside the live environment — at ICC T20 World Cup venues, on ISL and Pro Kabaddi League match floors, in Delhi Capitals sponsor servicing rooms — where the consequences of poor execution were immediate, measurable, and public.

Across every one of those environments the same pattern repeated: strong commercial ambition, insufficient operational architecture to support it. Sponsorship commitments signed before delivery systems existed. Venues planned without revenue models. Leagues launched without governance frameworks to survive year two.

That is what I fix. Not with theory. With frameworks built from having been inside the problem — not from having read about it.

Execution inside live sport is a different discipline.
There is no pause button. No revision cycle. No second delivery. The match starts. The sponsor activation either lands or it doesn't. The medal ceremony runs to plan or it doesn't. In those environments, you build the discipline to get it right first time, every time.
Sponsorship failure is almost always an operations problem.
Sponsors do not leave because the deal was bad. They leave because what was promised was not delivered. The fix is almost never commercial. It is operational. It is a systems problem. And systems problems have systems solutions.
Infrastructure built without commercial logic is capital at risk.
Across every sports infrastructure project I have seen, the decisions that cost the most were made before the commercial and operational frameworks existed. A venue without a programming strategy. A facility without a revenue model. Build the system first. Then the structure.
